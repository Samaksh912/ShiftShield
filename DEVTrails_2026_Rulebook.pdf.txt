 
 
The Economy - Your Survival Guide 
Welcome to Guidewire DEVTrails 2026: Unicorn Chase a 6-week startup simulation where you 
don't just build software, you run a company. 
Your team is a startup. You have DC (DEVTrails Coins) 1,00,000 in seed capital, a cloud bill that 
never stops growing, and investors who only fund winners. Your mission? Build an 
insurance-themed product, keep the lights on, and outsurvive everyone else. 
You'll receive an official Problem Statement, a real-world insurance challenge that your startup 
must solve. You're not building in the dark. The problem gives you direction; your job is to solve 
it and then innovate on top. The teams that just meet the brief get funded. The teams that 
reimagine it become Unicorns. 
And every Sunday at midnight, your weekly burn gets triggered. Every milestone, you pitch to 
investors for funding. Every phase, the bottom 25% of the leaderboard gets eliminated with no 
appeals, no second chances. And if your balance hits DC 0 at any point? You're bankrupt. Pack 
up. Game over. 
The top 5 teams that survive all six weeks make it to DemoJam at DevSummit 2026 (IPO Day) 
— the grand finale where the best startups go public and the Unicorns are crowned. 
This guide breaks down every way you earn money and every way you lose it. Read it. 
Memorize it. Your company's survival depends on it.

THE EARNS: How You Stay Alive 
Funding Rounds (The Big Money) 
At the end of each phase, you submit your product code, demo, and all. Then comes the 
moment of truth: a panel of investors tears through your architecture, UX, and logic to see if you 
actually built something worth backing. 
They will hand you a Star Rating that dictates the size of your funding cheque. Low stars? You 
are scraping by. Five stars? You just secured the bag. The difference between a pass and a 
powerhouse is not just a bonus; it is the difference between surviving the week and dominating 
the market.Funding is your lifeline. A consistent 3-star team earns DC 82,000 — just enough to 
cover the DC 75,000 burn. Anything less and you're bleeding cash.
